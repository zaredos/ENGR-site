Remaking My Nametag

For my latest 3D printing project, I decided to remake my nametag from scratch using TinkerCAD. Learning from my previous experience, I wanted to add more texture and life to the nametag, so I incorporated different objects like stars and scribbles. The design process was both fun and challenging as I experimented with shapes and arrangements, ultimately settling on a design with my name centered amidst the various elements.

After finalizing the design, I exported the model as an STL file and put it into PrusaSlicer to prepare the model for printing. I opted for a 20% infill and decided to print without supports or brim to simplify the process. Using the PRUSA Mini and Prusament PLA filament, the printing process took approximately 27 minutes.

The end result exceeded my expectations. The nametag turned out beautifully, with the added texture giving it a unique and vibrant appearance. Unlike my previous attempt, I encountered no issues during the printing process, and the nametag emerged flawlessly from the printer. Combination Print with Fusion360

For my second project, I delved into Fusion360 to create a combination print. I utilized the combine tool to combine a mesh of a phone stand and charging cable holders. The design incorporated two cable holders positioned near the bottom of the phone holder, allowing for convenient charging while being able to hold a phone in b both vertical and horizontal positions. After combining both and making sure that they were fully connected to each other, I used the merge function to combined the meshes into one to be ready for printing.

Combining the two meshes together

Merging the two meshes

After exporting the file as an STL, I imported it into PrusaSlicer for slicing. Similar to the nametag, I opted for a 20% infill and used organic tree supports since I've had good past experiences with easy removal. The printing process began in our classroom on Friday, with an estimated duration of 5 hours and around 55 grams of filament used.
